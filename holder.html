3.1 Terms and Definitions

Key Terms in Cyber Attribution

Digital Evidence: Information and data stored, received, or transmitted by an electronic device, valuable for an investigation. This includes data found on various devices and platforms such as computers, mobile phones, and online services (Vedwal, 2023).

Volatile Evidence: Information that changes frequently and is usually lost when a device is powered off, such as running processes or network contents (Vedwal, 2023).

Non-Volatile Evidence: Data that remains on an electronic device even when it is not powered on (Vedwal, 2023).

International Cyber Law (ICL)

Electronic Record: As defined under Section 2(t) of the IT Act, this includes data, record, or data generated, image or sound stored, received, or sent in an electronic form. It covers a wide range of electronic materials such as emails, digital photos, and word documents (Vedwal, 2023).

Admissibility of Electronic Evidence: This pertains to the rules and conditions under which electronic evidence is considered valid and can be used in court. This includes the importance of following standard operating procedures for evidence collection and the necessity of maintaining evidence integrity and authenticity (Vedwal, 2023).
